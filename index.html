EV Charger Installation Cost Guide

How much does EV charger installation cost in Australia? Here's an honest breakdown.

Single Phase (7.4kW)

$800 – $1,200

Most common for standard homes. Suits the majority of EV owners. Includes charger unit, wiring, and certification.

Three Phase (22kW)

$1,200 – $2,000

Faster charging for high-usage vehicles. Requires three-phase power at your property. Includes full installation and testing.

Factors That Affect Installation Cost

  • Distance from switchboard to garage
  • Age and condition of your switchboard
  • Single vs three-phase power supply
  • Wall material (brick, weatherboard, etc.)
  • Smart charging features required
  • Trenching for underground cable runs
  • Solar integration setup
  • State-based rebates and incentives available

Why Use a Licensed Electrician?

In Australia, EV charger installation must be carried out by a licensed electrician. Here's why it matters.

📜

It's the Law

Australian standards AS/NZS 3000 require all electrical work including EV charger installation to be performed by a licensed electrician.

🛡️

Insurance & Warranty

DIY or unlicensed installation voids your home insurance and charger warranty. Licensed work is fully insured and compliant.

🔥

Fire Safety

Incorrect wiring is a major fire risk. Licensed electricians size cables correctly, install proper protection, and certify the work.

📋

Compliance Certificate

You receive a Certificate of Electrical Safety — required for insurance claims, home sales, and grid-connected solar systems.

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about EV charger installation in Australia.

In most Australian states, a standard residential EV charger installation does not require council approval — only an electrical permit and compliance certificate from your licensed electrician. However, if significant structural work or underground trenching is involved, a building permit may be required. Your installer will advise you.

Most modern EVs (Tesla, BYD, Hyundai Ioniq, Kia EV6, MG, Volvo) work with standard Type 2 AC chargers. Tesla vehicles use a proprietary connector but Tesla Wall Connectors are widely supported. We'll match you with the right charger based on your vehicle make and model, home power supply, and budget.

Most standard residential EV charger installations take 2–4 hours on the day. If switchboard upgrades or additional cabling runs are required, allow 4–6 hours. We typically book jobs within 1–5 business days of your quote being approved, and same-week appointments are usually available.

Yes — several Australian states offer rebates and incentives. Victoria offers the ZEV Subsidy which includes charger installation support. The ACT has various EV incentives. Some electricity retailers also offer EV tariffs and rebates for smart charger installation. We'll advise you on what's available in your state at the time of your quote.

Single phase (240V) delivers up to 7.4kW — sufficient for overnight charging of most EVs, adding roughly 40–50km of range per hour. Three phase (415V) delivers up to 22kW but requires three-phase power at your property (common in newer homes and commercial premises). Not all EVs accept three-phase AC charging — your vehicle's onboard charger determines the maximum rate.

Yes — smart EV chargers like the Zappi and Fronius Wattpilot are designed to integrate with solar inverters and prioritise charging from excess solar generation. This can significantly reduce your charging costs. We can configure your charger to work with your existing solar system.

Homes built before 2000 often have older switchboards that may need upgrading before an EV charger can be safely installed. Our electricians assess your switchboard during the site visit and will quote any required upgrades upfront. Switchboard upgrades typically add $800–$2,000 to the job, depending on the age and condition of your existing board.

We install all major EV charger brands including Tesla Wall Connector, Zappi, Wallbox Pulsar, Fronius Wattpilot, ABB Terra, EVNEX, Clipper Creek, and all OCPP-compatible units. If you've already purchased a charger, we can install it. If you need a recommendation, we'll suggest the best option for your vehicle and home setup.
